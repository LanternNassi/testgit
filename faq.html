Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to apply for grants through Fund Vision Uganda?

Registered non-profit organizations, educational institutions, and civil society organizations working towards achieving SDG 4 targets in Uganda are eligible to apply. We encourage applications that focus on improving access to quality education, particularly for marginalized groups.

What types of projects does Fund Vision Uganda support?

We support a diverse range of projects aligned with SDG 4, including:

  • Building or renovating schools and classrooms
  • Providing teacher training and professional development
  • Developing and distributing educational materials
  • Promoting access to technology and digital learning resources
  • Supporting inclusive education initiatives for children with disabilities
How do I apply for a grant?

You can apply for grants electronically through our user-friendly online portal. Visit our Services page for more information and to access the application portal.

What is the grant review process like?

We have a transparent and merit-based grant review process. Applications are evaluated by a panel of experts based on their alignment with SDG 4 targets, project feasibility, and potential impact. All applicants receive notification of the review decision.

What are the reporting requirements for grant recipients?

Grantees are required to submit regular progress reports using our online reporting tools. These reports track project implementation, budget utilization, and impact on SDG 4 indicators. We provide templates and support to ensure smooth reporting.
